History

The Firefolio project has come a long way since it was first started in 2017. This timeline is intended to catch you up to speed on where it all came from, and where it might go next.

2017

November

Wednesday 15th

Callum is given an assignment to build a content management system by his university, which he initially thinks will be a small project for himself.

Friday 17th

Grads in Games holds their "Get in the Game" careers session at Callum's university. During the question and answers session at the end of the talk, and asks about a software solution. The speaker thinks it's an interesting concept, and Callum decides to expand the scope of his assignment to allow people other than himself to use it.

Development on the project codenamed Blue Carbon begins.

Tuesday 28th

The official Grads in Games Discord server is formed at Callum's request.

2018

Feburary

Wednesday 14th

After handing it in to his tutor, the release version of Blue Carbon is announced privately to the Grads in Games Discord server.

Wednesday 21st

Callum grows dissatisfied with the quality of Blue Carbon. He declares it a prototype, and begins work on the next version of the online portfolio system concept.

March

Monday 19th

Development of Firefolio is announced.

Sunday 25th

Callum finishes designing the "Burning Quill" logo to represent the project visually.

June

Monday 11th

Firefolio reaches feature completion and moves out of beta.

Thursday 21st

Firefolio v1.0.0 releases to the public.
